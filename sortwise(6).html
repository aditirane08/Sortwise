🌿 Bin guide

Your five bins, decoded

Every household and campus we've looked at uses some version of these five. Here's what actually goes where.

Green bin

Wet Waste

Anything that was alive or came out of the kitchen. Peels, leftovers, tea leaves, garden trimmings. This is what a compost pile actually wants.

Blue bin

Dry Waste

Paper, cardboard, and fabric that stays dry and won't rot. Newspapers, cereal boxes, old clothes that are better donated than dumped.

Yellow bin

Recyclable Waste

Bottles, jars, cans, and packaging that can genuinely go through a recycling plant. Give it a rinse first, leftover food ruins a whole batch.

Red bin

Hazardous Waste

Batteries, bulbs, old medicines, paint, anything electronic. Never toss these in with regular trash, they need a proper collection point.

Black bin

General Waste

What's left over. Broken ceramics, styrofoam, sanitary items, anything that can't be composted or recycled through normal channels.
